Our Story

I did it the hard way
for three years.
Then I built this.

Now Batting was built by a baseball dad who spent seasons collecting MP3s, editing audio tracks, and managing playlists — all so a bunch of 7-year-olds could have their walk-up song moment. It was worth it. But it didn't have to be that hard.

When my son started playing at 7U, I volunteered to do the music for the team. I didn't really think through what that meant.

I had to collect every kid's song request, track down the MP3 files, edit each one together with a voice clip, export them, load them onto my phone, save them to a playlist — and then before every single game, reorder the playlist to match that day's batting lineup. Start to finish, it took hours of work just to get ready.

"Every time I showed up, the kids asked if I was coming — because they wanted to make sure they got their walk-up music."

And you know what? The kids absolutely loved it. If you're a parent or a coach who's done the music before, you know exactly what I'm talking about. That moment when a kid steps up to the plate and their song drops — you can see it on their face. They stand a little taller. They smile. Their teammates go nuts. It's special.

I loved creating that feeling for the kids. I loved being the reason those memories got made. But man, it sure took a lot of work to build, to set up before each game, and to actually run on game day.

And if I couldn't make it to a game? Forget about it. No one else could do it. Too complicated to hand off. The kids just didn't get their music that day.

This year, I knew there had to be a better way. I looked for apps. I found a few — but they were hard to operate. Too technical. Too many settings and options. I just wanted something super simple, clean, and reliable. Something I could trust to work every time. And something I could easily hand off to another parent if I had to miss a game — without them needing to log into an account or worry about whether the field had wifi.

Nothing like that existed. So I built it.

Now Batting is that app. Excited to bring it to the world.

— Andrew, Dad & Founder

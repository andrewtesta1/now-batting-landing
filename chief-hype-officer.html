The Chief Hype Officer.

For the one who volunteered to run the music

The Definition

Who is the Chief Hype Officer?

The Chief Hype Officer is the parent — or coach — who volunteered to run the walk-up music.

Who said yes before reading the job description.

Who pays for Apple Music every month now. Just for this.

Who has opinions about drop points.

Who showed up with a lawn chair, a wagon, and a Bluetooth speaker the size of a small appliance.

Who drinks cold coffee because they were too busy getting the lineup right.

Who knows every kid's song. Who knows Calvin wants "Enter Sandman" at exactly fourteen seconds — because Calvin told them. Four times.

Who felt the panic — shuffling through the playlist right before the next walk-up, everybody watching — and did it anyway.

Who keeps showing up — again — because those kids deserve someone who does.

They are the reason the dugout goes nuts.

Now Batting was built for them.

The Manifesto

You already have enough going on.

The mental load that follows you everywhere — including a youth baseball complex at 8:45am on a Saturday, where you are now also responsible for the music.

Nobody asked you to do this.

And yet — there you are.

Because these kids deserved better than silence. Because you knew — if you could pull this off — they'd feel like big leaguers walking to the plate.

You are the Chief Hype Officer.

No salary. No training. No one coming to save you.

Just one job: make the moment.

We see you.

Phone in one hand. Coffee in the other. One eye on the game. Squinting through polarized sunglasses at a screen cranked to maximum brightness.

Lawn chair. Wagon. Bluetooth speaker the size of a small appliance.

We built this for you.

The Saturday Standard.

"Will this make life easier for the parent running the music?"

They show up every Saturday.

And the parents in those folding chairs? They have no idea how you did it.

That's not luck.

That's a Chief Hype Officer doing their job.

And an app that finally showed up for you.

Yes — here's how to make it seamless:

1. Get your roster ready first. Make sure every player has their walk-up song set, their announcement generated, and their batting order locked in. The person you're sharing with will get exactly what you have.

2. Tap "Share Roster." In the app, tap your team name at the top, then hit Share Roster. You'll get a short link and an 8-character code.

3. Send the link. If they already have Now Batting installed, the roster imports automatically — one tap and they're in. If they don't have the app yet, they'll see the full lineup on the web. Once they download Now Batting, they enter the 8-character code and the entire roster — songs, timestamps, announcements — loads instantly on their phone.

They're ready to run game day exactly like you would.
Yes — but you need to do three things before you get to the field:

1. Generate each player's announcement. Open each player card in the app and tap the microphone button to generate their announcement. Once it's generated, it's saved to your phone forever.

2. Set your song timestamps. On each player's song screen, drag the marker to the exact moment you want the music to kick in, then save. This is what makes it feel automatic on game day.

3. Download your songs in Apple Music. Open the Apple Music app, find each walk-up song, tap the three-dot menu, and select Download. Downloaded songs play back with no signal needed.

Once those three things are done, Now Batting runs completely offline. No WiFi, no cell signal, no problem.
